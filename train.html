80/20 Polarized Training
The 80% Zone 2, 20% high intensity split is based on polarized training research. Endurance athletes who train predominantly at low intensity with a small amount of high intensity outperform those who train at moderate intensity across the board. The mechanism is mitochondrial density and aerobic enzyme adaptation without accumulating fatigue.
Seiler, S. & Tønnessen, E. (2009). Intervals, thresholds, and long slow distance: the role of intensity and duration in endurance training. Sportscience, 13, 32–53.
Concurrent Training
Cutting strength sessions from 5–7 to 3 during this phase reduces interference effect, the phenomenon where excessive strength volume blunts endurance adaptation. Three sessions per week maintains muscle mass and strength without competing with aerobic development.
Wilson, J.M. et al. (2012). Concurrent training: a meta-analysis examining interference of aerobic and resistance exercises. Journal of Strength and Conditioning Research, 26(8), 2293–2307.

Why This Block Matters Most

Two-a-Days and Recovery

Why Two-A-Days Work Here
Two-a-day training sessions are effective when recovery infrastructure is in place. Home cooking means hitting protein and carb targets consistently. Home bed means real sleep. No work commute or internship obligations means training can be structured around recovery windows, not around a calendar. This is the exact environment where two-a-days produce adaptation rather than breakdown.
Trail Running Advantage
Nashville greenway trail running builds proprioception, ankle stability, and glute activation that road running does not. The uneven surface recruits stabilizer muscles and reduces impact force per stride, which means lower injury risk despite higher volume. Use this window to build trail mileage before returning to Bloomington roads.
MD Access as a Training Asset
Any soft tissue issue, unusual fatigue, or pain that changes gait gets looked at immediately rather than trained through. Two physicians at home means nothing festers for two weeks before getting attention. Use this. It is a genuine competitive advantage most athletes do not have.

Long Run Pacing
Running 60–90 seconds per mile slower than goal marathon pace on long runs optimizes aerobic adaptation while minimizing injury risk and recovery time. The purpose of the long run is time on feet and fat oxidation training, not speed development.
Daniels, J. (2005). Daniels' Running Formula (2nd ed.). Human Kinetics.
Pre-Long Run Carb Loading
Carbohydrate loading the night before every long run and race week saturates muscle glycogen stores. The target of 2.7–4.5g carbs per lb bodyweight is supported by multiple studies showing significant performance benefits for efforts over 90 minutes.
Burke, L.M. et al. (2011). Carbohydrates for training and competition. Journal of Sports Sciences, 29(S1), S17–S27.

Post-Marathon Recovery Window
Full physiological recovery from a marathon takes 3–4 weeks minimum. Muscle damage, glycogen depletion, and immune suppression all require time. The 2-week no-running rule in weeks 1–2 of this phase is based on this. Returning before that increases injury risk significantly.
Petersen, J. et al. (2007). Running mechanics and injury risk. Sports Medicine.
HYROX as Strength Stimulus
HYROX station work serves a dual purpose here: race completion and functional strength stimulus for Augusta. The sled, sandbag lunges, and wall balls specifically target the posterior chain and leg endurance that will matter on the Augusta bike and run.

Brick Workouts
Bike-to-run training develops the neuromuscular adaptation needed to run efficiently on fatigued legs. The body learns to recruit running-specific muscle fibers while still flushing lactic acid from the cycling effort. This is the most race-specific session in the whole plan.
Why Augusta
Augusta 70.3 is one of the fastest 70.3 courses in North America for a reason. The Savannah River swim is downstream and current-assisted, making the 1.2 mile swim significantly more manageable for a first 70.3. The bike is rolling but not technical. The run is flat. The course is designed for people doing their first half ironman to have a good experience.
Deload Periodization
Deloading every 3rd–4th week allows supercompensation, the physiological process where the body adapts to training stress during recovery and comes back stronger. Training without deloads plateaus fitness and accumulates fatigue without net gain.
Issurin, V.B. (2010). New horizons for the methodology and physiology of training periodization. Sports Medicine, 40(3), 189–206.
