Why These Paces: VDOT Framework
Your 4:07 LA Marathon corresponds to approximately VDOT 38–39. Your 3:30–3:45 goal requires VDOT 44–46, a 6–8 point improvement. The paces in this plan are derived from Daniels' VDOT equations and calibrated to your current fitness, not your goal fitness. Running at goal pace now when your VDOT doesn't support it leads to chronic fatigue and injury, not adaptation. The paces will get faster as VDOT improves across phases.
Daniels, J. (2014). Daniels' Running Formula, 3rd Edition. Human Kinetics.
Why Threshold Runs Every Week
Threshold pace (8:40–8:55/mi at your current VDOT) is the single most effective session for marathon performance. It raises your lactate threshold, the pace at which lactate accumulates faster than you can clear it. Previous plans alternated threshold and intervals every other week. That means roughly one hard quality session per 14 days. Daniels and Pfitzinger both show weekly threshold work is the minimum effective dose for meaningful marathon improvement. The previous plan was not enough.
Why Strides After Easy Runs
Strides (4×20 second controlled accelerations after easy runs) develop neuromuscular speed and running economy without accumulating aerobic fatigue. Research consistently shows that runners who include strides maintain faster leg turnover at all paces. They take 3 minutes. They are not optional.
Concurrent Training and Strength
Three gym sessions during Barcelona balances strength maintenance against endurance adaptation. Excessive strength volume blunts the mitochondrial signaling that endurance training creates. Dropping to zero loses muscle critical for running economy and HYROX performance. Three sessions is the evidence-based sweet spot for concurrent training.
Wilson, J.M. et al. (2012). Concurrent training: a meta-analysis. Journal of Strength and Conditioning Research, 26(8), 2293–2307.

Why Two-A-Days Work Here
Two-a-day sessions are productive only when recovery is adequate. Home food, a real kitchen hitting protein targets, consistent sleep in a familiar bed, and no internship stress means the recovery conditions here are better than any other phase. That is what makes the double sessions viable. The same schedule in Barcelona or during ICORE would lead to breakdown.
Swim Progression for Untrained Swimmers
Costill et al. established that untrained swimmers need 6–8 weeks of technique-first work before volume meaningfully improves fitness. Starting at 800m in short sets and building to 1,800m over 5 weeks follows this. The risk of jumping to 2,000m+ immediately is compensatory stroke mechanics that cause shoulder impingement, one of the most common triathlon injuries and one of the slowest to heal.
Costill, D.L. et al. (1991). Adaptations to swimming training: influence of training volume. Medicine and Science in Sports and Exercise, 23(3), 371–377.
MD Access as a Training Asset
Any soft tissue issue, unusual fatigue, or pain that changes gait gets looked at immediately rather than trained through. Two physicians at home means nothing festers. Use this. It is a genuine competitive advantage.

Long Run Pacing
Running 60–90 seconds per mile slower than goal marathon pace on long runs optimizes aerobic adaptation while minimizing injury risk and recovery time. The purpose of the long run is time on feet and fat oxidation training, not speed development.
Daniels, J. (2005). Daniels' Running Formula (2nd ed.). Human Kinetics.
Pre-Long Run Carb Loading
Carbohydrate loading the night before every long run and race week saturates muscle glycogen stores. The target of 2.7–4.5g carbs per lb bodyweight is supported by multiple studies showing significant performance benefits for efforts over 90 minutes.
Burke, L.M. et al. (2011). Carbohydrates for training and competition. Journal of Sports Sciences, 29(S1), S17–S27.

Post-Marathon Recovery Window
Full physiological recovery from a marathon takes 3–4 weeks minimum. Muscle damage, glycogen depletion, and immune suppression all require time. The 2-week no-running rule in weeks 1–2 of this phase is based on this. Returning before that increases injury risk significantly.
Petersen, J. et al. (2007). Running mechanics and injury risk. Sports Medicine.
HYROX as Strength Stimulus
HYROX station work serves a dual purpose here: race completion and functional strength stimulus for Augusta. The sled, sandbag lunges, and wall balls specifically target the posterior chain and leg endurance that will matter on the Augusta bike and run.

Brick Workouts
Bike-to-run training develops the neuromuscular adaptation needed to run efficiently on fatigued legs. The body learns to recruit running-specific muscle fibers while still flushing lactic acid from the cycling effort. This is the most race-specific session in the whole plan.
Why Augusta
Augusta 70.3 is one of the fastest 70.3 courses in North America for a reason. The Savannah River swim is downstream and current-assisted, making the 1.2 mile swim significantly more manageable for a first 70.3. The bike is rolling but not technical. The run is flat. The course is designed for people doing their first half ironman to have a good experience.
Deload Periodization
Deloading every 3rd–4th week allows supercompensation, the physiological process where the body adapts to training stress during recovery and comes back stronger. Training without deloads plateaus fitness and accumulates fatigue without net gain.
Issurin, V.B. (2010). New horizons for the methodology and physiology of training periodization. Sports Medicine, 40(3), 189–206.
